# Accel Vibration Plot Guide

Quick reference for interpreting each plot generated by the vibration analyzer.

---

## accel_psd.png — Power Spectral Density

Shows the frequency content of raw accelerometer vibration (pre-filter) during hover.

- **Left column**: Full frequency range. Look for the overall shape — energy should roll off at higher frequencies.
- **Right column**: Zoomed 0-500 Hz. This is where motor vibration peaks live.
- **Red dashed lines**: Motor RPM and its integer multiples (2x, 3x, 4x). Vibration peaks near these lines are motor-driven and can be targeted by the ESC RPM dynamic notch filter.
- **Dark red annotations**: Detected PSD peaks with their frequency.

**What to look for:**
- Peaks that align with motor harmonic lines → ESC RPM DNF will handle these
- Peaks that DON'T align → possible structural resonances, candidates for static notch filters
- Broad energy humps → harder to filter, may indicate mechanical isolation issues

---

## accel_spectrogram.png — Z-Axis Spectrogram

Shows how the vibration spectrum changes over time, with motor RPM overlay.

- **Top panel**: Spectrogram (bright = more energy at that frequency/time). The inferno colormap ranges from dark (quiet) to bright yellow (loud).
- **Blue lines**: Motor frequency and its multiples, tracked from ESC RPM telemetry.
- **Middle panel**: Motor RPM over time.
- **Bottom panel**: Post-filter accel vibration metric over time.

**What to look for:**
- Bright lines that follow the blue motor lines → motor-driven vibration (DNF target)
- Bright lines that stay at a fixed frequency regardless of RPM → structural resonances (static notch target)
- Energy between harmonic lines → broadband vibration, harder to notch-filter
- Correlation between RPM changes and vibration metric spikes

---

## z_velocity.png — Z Velocity Estimation Quality

Compares EKF vertical velocity (driven by accelerometer) against range sensor derivative (ground truth). Both signals are detrended (high-pass filtered) to remove baro-induced drift and isolate accel performance.

- **Top panel**: Range sensor altitude for flight context.
- **Middle panel**: Detrended EKF Vz vs detrended range sensor Vz. If the accel is clean, these should overlap closely.
- **Bottom panel**: Two error signals:
  - **Black — Dynamic error**: High-frequency difference between EKF and range sensor. This is vibration-induced noise on the accel. Lower is better. *This is what notch filters improve.*
  - **Red — Accel bias drift**: Low-frequency difference. This is vibration rectification error (high-freq vibration aliasing into a DC accel offset). Appears as a slowly-wandering velocity bias.

**Metrics:**
- **Dynamic RMS** < 0.1 m/s = good, 0.1-0.3 = moderate, > 0.3 = high vibration noise
- **Bias RMS** < 0.1 m/s = good, 0.1-0.3 = moderate, > 0.3 = significant rectification error

---

## vibration_summary.png — Overview

Quick-look plot combining vibration metrics and ESC RPM.

- **Top panel**: Post-filter accel and gyro vibration metrics over time.
  - Accel: < 1.0 good, 1-3 moderate, > 3 high
  - Gyro: < 0.02 good, 0.02-0.05 moderate, > 0.05 high
- **Bottom panel**: Per-motor RPM from ESC telemetry. Look for RPM spread between motors (indicates imbalanced props or motors).
- **Params box**: Current filter parameters for reference.
